>> 01 / 03 · The participants

Validators and nominators — different jobs

VALIDATORS
Responsibilities:
Run a full node 24/7, produce relay chain blocks, verify parachain proofs, participate in GRANDPA finality voting.
Requirements:
Technical expertise, high uptime, large self-stake.
Active set: ~300 at a time
NOMINATORS
Responsibilities:
Research validators, bond DOT behind trustworthy ones (up to 16).
Requirements:
No technical setup, minimum ~250 DOT to nominate.
~50,000+ nominators currently backing the validator set
Risk is shared. If your nominated validator gets slashed, you lose a proportional share of your stake. Choose validators with good uptime and reasonable commission.

>> 02 / 03 · The election

Why Polkadot balances stake — not just picks the richest

A naive system would just pick the validators with the most DOT staked. Problem: 10 validators with 90% of all stake means huge centralisation. One cartel controls Polkadot.

NAIVE: PICK TOP STAKERS
  • The 300 richest validators always win.
  • Their nominators get all rewards. Nobody else bothers.
  • Stake concentrates. Network becomes centralised.
NPOS: PHRAGMÉN ALGORITHM
  • Tries to spread stake evenly across all elected validators.
  • A validator with less total DOT can win if spreading their backers' stake increases overall distribution.
  • More decentralised, fairer rewards.
The goal: Every elected validator should have roughly equal stake backing them. This prevents any single validator (or cartel) from having disproportionate influence.

>> 03 / 03 · The rewards

How rewards flow from validators to nominators

At the end of each 24-hour era, the protocol calculates rewards for each validator based on the number of blocks they produced and the fees they collected. The validator takes their commission (e.g. 5%), and the rest is split proportionally among all nominators backing that validator.

Low commission ≠ best choice. A validator with 0% commission but frequent downtime earns fewer rewards than a 5% commission validator with perfect uptime. Always check the validator's performance history, not just commission.

>> The deterrent

Slashing — graduated penalties for misconduct

Offence Slash Amount Who Loses
Being offline / unresponsive Small % (0.1%) Validator only
Equivocation (double-signing) Up to 100% Validator + all nominators
Coordinated attack Up to 100% Validator + all nominators
Nominators share slashing risk. This is why nomination is not passive — you must monitor your validators. Polkadot.js apps shows a validator's history; check for past slash events before nominating.
