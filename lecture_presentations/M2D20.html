>> Design first

What does a Word Counter measure?

Three numbers from one block of text. Each has a one-line trick you already know.

Characters

Just the length of the string.

len(text)

Words

Split on whitespace, count the pieces.

len(text.split())

Lines

Split on the newline character.

len(text.splitlines())

New friend: .splitlines() is just .split()'s cousin — it breaks on line-breaks (\n) instead of spaces.
>> The core, in full

The whole counter

word_counter.py
text = input("Paste your text: ")

chars = len(text)
words = len(text.split())
lines = len(text.splitlines())

print(f"Characters: {chars}")
print(f"Words:      {words}")
print(f"Lines:      {lines}")

# Bonus — the longest word, using a list + sort:
longest = sorted(text.split(), key=len)[-1]
print(f"Longest word: {longest}")
EVERY SKILL len, .split(), f-strings, sorted, and slicing [-1] — all in one tidy program.
