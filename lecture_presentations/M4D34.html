>> 01 / 03 · Overview

One token, three jobs

01 STAKING

Lock DOT to become a validator or nominator. Earn rewards (~15% APY). Secure the network. Lose a portion if your validator misbehaves.

02 GOVERNANCE

Vote on network changes. Runtime upgrades. Treasury spending. Fee parameters. 1 DOT = 1 vote (conviction multiplied by lock time).

03 BONDING

Teams lock DOT to reserve a parachain slot (old model) or buy Coretime (new model). The locked DOT is returned when the period ends.

Total supply: ~1.3 billion DOT. About 50% is currently staked. The staking rewards come from inflation — the protocol mints new DOT to pay validators and nominators.
>> 02 / 03 · Staking

How staking works in practice

You don't need to run a validator node to earn staking rewards. As a nominator, you back existing validators with your DOT.

BECOMING A VALIDATOR
REQUIRES

Technical setup (server + node software), minimum self-stake (varies, currently ~millions of DOT to be competitive), running 24/7.

EARNS

Block rewards + transaction fees

RISKS

Slashing if misbehave

BECOMING A NOMINATOR
REQUIRES

Any amount of DOT (minimum ~250 DOT currently), choose up to 16 validators to back, no technical setup.

EARNS

Share of validators' rewards

RISKS

Share of validators' slashing

The era system: Polkadot runs in 24-hour "eras." At the end of each era, rewards are distributed and the validator set may rotate. Your nominations take effect at the start of the next era.
>> 03 / 03 · Governance

OpenGov — anyone can propose, everyone can vote

Polkadot has fully on-chain governance. Any DOT holder can submit a proposal. Proposals that pass get automatically enacted — no company decision needed. Polkadot has upgraded itself dozens of times this way.

  1. Any DOT holder submits a referendum (a proposed change)
  2. A voting period opens — DOT holders vote Aye or Nay
  3. Conviction voting: lock your DOT longer = your vote counts more (up to 6×)
  4. If passed with enough support, the change is automatically enacted on-chain
Real example: The transition from Slot Auctions to Coretime was approved by a governance vote in 2023. No Polkadot company could block it — the community voted and it happened.
>> Live tools

Polkadot.js apps — the Swiss Army knife

polkadot.js.org/apps is the official interface for interacting with Polkadot. Think of it as the bank's website — for a bank you control.

ACCOUNTS

See your DOT balance, send transactions, view transaction history. Connect with the Polkadot.js browser extension.

STAKING

View all active validators, their total stake, commission rates, and nomination counts. Bond DOT and nominate validators.

GOVERNANCE

Browse all active referenda. See voting status, turnout, and whether proposals are passing. Vote with your DOT.

EXPLORER

See live blocks, recent extrinsics (transactions), and events. Similar to Subscan but more technical detail.
