>> 01 / 04 · The concept

A parachain is a blockchain with a superpower

A parachain is a full blockchain — it has its own tokens, its own rules, its own smart contracts. The superpower: it connects to the Polkadot relay chain, which means it gets Polkadot's validators to finalize its blocks. It doesn't need its own validator set.

The analogy: The relay chain is a country's military. Parachains are cities in that country. Each city governs itself — but they all share the country's military for defense. A small city doesn't need to maintain its own army.
STANDALONE CHAIN
  • Has its own validators
  • Needs to recruit and fund them
  • Vulnerable if small
  • All security cost on you
PARACHAIN
  • Uses Polkadot's validators
  • Zero security setup cost
  • Protected by full DOT stake
  • Can focus entirely on building useful features
>> 02 / 04 · The connection

Collators, validators, and the proof

Parachains have their own node type — Collators. They build parachain blocks and submit a proof to the relay chain validators, who verify and finalize them.

01 COLLATOR BUILDS

The collator assembles parachain transactions into a block and creates a Proof of Validity (PoV).

02 VALIDATOR CHECKS

A subset of relay chain validators verifies the PoV. They check signatures and state transitions.

03 RELAY CHAIN FINALIZES

The block is included in the relay chain. GRANDPA finalizes it. Now it's irreversible.

Collators vs validators: Collators are parachain-specific and don't need to stake DOT. Validators are relay chain nodes with large DOT stakes. Different roles, different requirements.

>> 04 / 04 · Access model

How parachains pay for relay chain access

OLD MODEL: SLOT AUCTIONS
  • Teams bid DOT in 2-year slot auctions
  • Only ~50 slots available
  • Winners lock up millions of DOT
  • Expensive and inflexible
NEW MODEL: CORETIME (2024+)
  • Teams buy "Coretime" — compute time on the relay chain
  • Like buying cloud credits
  • Pay for what you need
  • A weekend hackathon project can rent coretime for a few days
Why this matters: Coretime makes Polkadot accessible to small teams and experiments. You don't need millions of DOT to try building a parachain. The ecosystem is more open.
>> Big picture

Polkadot vs Ethereum — different visions

Aspect Ethereum Polkadot
Application layer Smart contracts on one chain Entire parachains, each specialized
Scaling Layer 2 rollups (Optimism, Arbitrum) Multiple parachains in parallel
Communication Bridges (complex, often hacked) Native XCM between parachains
Neither is "better." Ethereum has a larger developer ecosystem. Polkadot has more architectural flexibility. Many projects bridge both.
